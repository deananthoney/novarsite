Selective costimulation blockade
Belatacept selectively binds to CD80 and CD86, blocking the interaction with CD2811
The result: T-cell apoptosis and prevention
of T-cell—mediated rejection11
A quick look back at how
belatacept differs from other
immunosuppressive agents
  • Basiliximab binds to and blocks the
    interleukin 2 receptor (IL-2R) on activated T
    cells, thus inhibiting IL-2—induced T-cell
    proliferation13
  • CNIs block the rate-limiting protein
    phosphatase, calcineurin, needed for the signal
    1 transduction pathway13
  • MPA inhibits inosine monophosphate
    dehydrogenase (IMPDH), the rate-limiting
    enzyme in purine synthesis13
    • Sirolimus and everolimus inhibit the protein
    kinase, mTOR, blocking downstream IL-2
    effects and inhibiting cell-cycle progression13
